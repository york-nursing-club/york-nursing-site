Start Here — New Student Guide

You've been admitted — here's the order of operations for health clearance, uniforms, and exam setup, plus the mistakes that cost past students time and money.

Official source for all deadlines Health clearance requirements are administered through CastleBranch and the Department of Nursing, not the Club. This page is a plain-language guide — always confirm exact current deadlines with the department.

1. Orientation

Mandatory. Typically held in June or July before your first fall semester. Watch your York email for the exact date/time — it usually arrives with your admission packet.

2. Health Clearance — CastleBranch

Set up your CastleBranch account as soon as you're admitted. Cost is $94.18, package code UQ29DTIM.

Fee heads-up: the printed handbook says $89.98 and the live Undergrad Policies page says $65.00 — neither is current. $94.18 is the confirmed actual fee as of this writing. Double-check with CastleBranch/the department before paying, since fees do change.

Full checklist

  • MMR — 2 vaccinations, or a positive antibody titer for all 3 components
  • Varicella — 2 vaccinations, or a positive antibody titer
  • Hepatitis B — 3 vaccinations, positive titer, or a signed declination waiver
  • Tuberculosis — annual renewal: 1-Step TB skin test, QuantiFERON Gold blood test, or chest x-ray if a prior test was positive
  • Tetanus (Tdap) — renews every 10 years
  • CPR Certification — must specifically be the AHA Healthcare Provider course. Upload front and back of the signed card.
  • Physical Exam — official school form (see Forms Library), signed and stamped by a physician/NP/PA. Renews annually by June 15.
  • Title IX Sexual Harassment Training — certificate of completion
  • FIT Test — respirator fit test results
  • COVID-19 vaccination — documentation must include the manufacturer
  • Influenza — required annually Aug–March, or a declination waiver. Must include lot number and expiration date.
  • Infection Control Certification — required per the live application page; not listed in the printed handbook, so easy to miss

Deadlines

  • Generic BS students: documents due 3rd week of July, for fall start
  • RN-BS students: documents due 4th week of November, for spring start
  • Drug test: no earlier than 2 weeks before classes start, but at least 1 day before
⭐ Immunization non-compliance = a WA grade. If your CastleBranch documents aren't complete by the college's compliance date, you can be issued a WA grade for the semester. This date moves every semester — check the current one on the Academic Calendar page before assuming last semester's date still applies.

3. Uniforms

Ordered through Meridy's Uniforms using passcode York2002. See the Policy Hub for the full uniform and grooming standards (Generic vs. RN-BS requirements differ).

4. NEX Exam — Setup Warning

⚠️ Set your account up correctly the first time. At account setup on the NLN Testing Portal, you must enter York's institution code: 476695-01 – York College – Queens. If you set it up wrong, your results won't connect to York, and you may be forced into a paid retake to fix it.
  • Cost: $80, purchased through the NLN Testing Portal
  • 90-day window to take the exam once purchased
  • Fully remote now — no longer offered at York's Testing Center
  • 30-day mandatory wait before a retake if you fail
  • ADA accommodations: NLN no longer processes accommodation requests directly. Instead, purchase the "ACC version" of the test (still $80) for extended time.
  • Questions: NLN Testing Office, (718) 262-2054, M–F 9AM–5PM
