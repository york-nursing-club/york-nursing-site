Tools & Platforms Glossary

Every one of these trips someone up every semester — not because they're complicated, but because nobody explains them up front. This page exists to cut down on the same questions getting asked over and over.

A student-to-student guide, not an official document This page reflects what students have generally figured out about these tools over time. Costs, grading weight, and access details vary by course and instructor — confirm specifics with your instructor when in doubt.
Prep U (adaptive quiz mastery — part of CoursePoint)

What it is: an adaptive quiz system bundled into Pearson CoursePoint, used for chapter-by-chapter mastery practice.

Where to find it: CoursePoint → select your course (e.g. Med-Surg) → sidebar → "Prep U" → select the chapter.

Why progress "doesn't move": the mastery level you need to hit isn't a fixed program-wide number — it varies by course and instructor (one course might require Level 4, another something different). Assigned chapter ranges also sometimes go beyond what's technically listed in the syllabus, since instructors adjust them often. Don't assume last semester's requirement carries over — confirm with your current instructor.

Kaplan (NCLEX readiness)

What it is: NCLEX-readiness testing, used every semester. Typically worth 5–10% of your course grade, depending on the course.

Cost: free — the school has a grant covering it. If someone tells you that you need to pay for it, that's wrong.

Access: comes via a link in a school email. If you don't have it, contact your instructor — don't assume the course doesn't use it.

Extended-time accommodations: students with extended time see the same countdown timer as everyone else, but get a "Continue" option instead of a forced "Submit" once the standard time expires — the extra time only becomes visible after the original time runs out. This hasn't been officially confirmed by the department, so double check if it matters for you.

Other notes: retakes are faculty-proctored. The "decision tree" video modules just need to be watched and marked complete — they're not tested separately. Login issues are a recurring complaint; if Kaplan won't let you log in, try clearing your browser cache before assuming something's broken on your account.

DocuCare (EHR simulation)

What it is: an electronic health record (EHR) simulation platform.

Cost: around $170.

Buy the right duration. DocuCare comes in 6/12/24-month options. Since it's used across multiple semesters and courses, a short-duration purchase risks expiring mid-program — think about how many semesters you'll actually need it for before you buy.

Access codes are instructor-specific — make sure you get the code for your exact lab section, not just any code circulating around.

ShadowHealth (virtual patient simulation)

What it is: virtual patient simulation software — you'll likely encounter the "Tina Jones" case and HEENT assessments.

Access: requires a 16-digit course pin that's specific to your course and instructor.

Free trial vs. buying directly: ShadowHealth has a free trial option, but some students buy access directly instead. Students who buy directly sometimes can't find the same assignments (like the Tina Jones case) that trial users see automatically — if that happens to you, contact your instructor rather than assuming something's wrong on your end.

Grade weight isn't consistently documented — confirm with your instructor.

vSim (clinical simulation / makeup option)

What it is: virtual clinical simulation software, used specifically as a clinical makeup/replacement option if you miss a clinical day. This isn't the same as Prep U, Kaplan, DocuCare, or ShadowHealth — it's tied to attendance, not coursework practice.

There's a "Student vSim Clinical Replacement Packet" (about 23 pages) that walks through the process — see the Forms Library for status on getting a copy posted here.

CircleIn

Correcting a common assumption: CircleIn is a general study-collaboration app, but in practice at York it is not used as an academic study tool. The Nursing Club uses it specifically to keep a membership roster/directory. If you were told to join CircleIn and expected course content there, that's why you're not finding any — it's a club organizing tool here, not a study platform.

Brightspace

What it is: York's learning management system (LMS) — syllabi, grades, and course-specific postings.

Where it matters most: required training videos (e.g. PPE, hand hygiene) get posted here on a course-specific basis. If you're not sure whether a required training video has gone up yet, this is where to check — not a school-wide announcement.
