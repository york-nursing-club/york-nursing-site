Attendance & Lateness Policy

This policy promotes professionalism, accountability, and excellence in patient care by ensuring nursing students attend all scheduled classes, clinical rotations, and other required educational activities. Absence from lecture, lab, or clinical is not permitted and may result in failure of the course.

  • Clinical/Campus Labs: absence is not permitted and will result in course failure. You can submit a letter of appeal to the Nursing APR Committee with proof of the event (see Forms Library).
  • Lecture: absences are not permitted. If you miss one, it's on you to get the content covered.
  • Attendance is taken at the start of each class period — you're marked absent if not present when it's taken.
  • All assignments are due on the due date. Cell phones/pagers must be off in the classroom, and recording lectures is prohibited.

Lateness

Lateness to lecture, lab, and clinical is not tolerated.

  • You must be on time to lecture, lab, and clinical.
  • If you're late, the professor will not admit you — this counts as an absence for the day.
  • Don't schedule outside appointments during class, lab, or clinical hours.
  • A medical emergency causing lateness requires a doctor's note for admittance.

Clinical Expectations

  • Proof of current physical exam, up-to-date immunization titers, and a current AHA CPR card must be on file with the Nursing Program.
  • Arrive on time and in the proper York College uniform.
  • You may be removed from the clinical area and/or the program for unsafe or irresponsible behavior — including administering medications without the clinical instructor present, not following instructor directions, or compromising patient safety.
  • The external clinical agency has the sole right to remove or bar entry to any student it believes poses a risk. Misconduct resulting in removal can lead to further disciplinary action, including dismissal from the program.
Uniform Policy (Generic + RN-BS, grooming standards)

Uniforms are required for all on-campus labs and clinical experiences.

Generic Nursing Program

  • Purchased from Meridy's Uniforms (meridys.com) using passcode York2002. You'll get a York College Nursing patch for the left upper sleeve.
  • White hosiery/socks and clean white nursing shoes. Anything worn underneath should be white — a plain (no logo), long-sleeved white crew or turtleneck is fine under the top.
  • No lab coats or other garments over the uniform except the white jewel-neck warm-up jacket (also from Meridy's).

RN-BS Program

  • White uniform top and black scrub pants with black shoes for Community Health clinical rotations and school-sponsored clinical events. Same warm-up jacket rule as above.
  • Clean white lab coat during NURS 203 on-campus labs.

All Programs — Grooming

  • Hair pulled back, away from the face and off the shoulders. Moustaches/beards neatly trimmed.
  • Minimal makeup, no fake eyelashes, no heavy makeup.
  • No jewelry except a wedding band and small post earrings — no necklaces, bracelets, or dangling earrings.
  • Nails trimmed with clear/pale polish only — no enhancements or extensions.
  • No perfume. No hats/head coverings unless for religious purposes. Tattoos must be covered.
  • No visible facial piercings (a religious waiver may be requested).
  • York College photo ID badge visible at all times.
Failure to comply results in a clinical absence for that day.

Real logistics (from cohort experience, not the handbook)

  • The uniform is worn only on lab/clinical days — not regular lecture days, unless a lab happens to fall on the same day. This is a recurring point of confusion every semester.
  • Sizing across Meridy's uniform pieces runs inconsistent — try items on before committing to sizes for a full set.
  • Grooming checks are enforced strictly in practice: white socks get checked, no insignias, pants/hem length enforced.
  • Whites visibly yellowing by semester 3 is normal wear from repeated washing — not a compliance issue on its own.

INC Grades

If extenuating circumstances cause you to miss the final while passing the course, you have 24 hours after the exam to contact your faculty member about an INC. If approved, you get up to 3 weeks next semester to take the final. If you took the final but didn't finish coursework, the Program Director/Chairperson can grant an INC with up to 10 weeks next semester to resolve it. Unresolved INCs convert to FIN, which counts as an F.

Progression Policy

  • Generic track: complete all Gen Ed/pathway and prerequisite courses, 3.0 GPA across BIO 234, BIO 235, CHEM 106/107, ENG 125, PSY 102, and a minimum 50th percentile on the NLN-PAX/NEX exam.
  • RN-BS track: complete all Gen Ed/pathway and prerequisite courses, minimum C+ in NURS 203, and 80%+ on the NURS 200 pharmacology challenge exam.
  • All prerequisite/Gen Ed courses need a minimum grade of C. Missed it once? You can appeal for one repeat — the new and old grades get averaged together.
  • Earning below C+ (77) in two required nursing courses in the same term, or across your time in the program, results in automatic dismissal.
  • You're allowed one withdrawal from a nursing course while in the program. A second withdrawal, or failing to earn C+ in the repeated course, means automatic dismissal.
  • Below C+ in a nursing course? You must meet with the Chairperson within 10 days to discuss next steps.

Dismissal Policy

Failing two required nursing courses below C+ (77) results in dismissal for all Generic and RN-BS students.

Professional misconduct — referred to the Undergraduate Program Director and can result in dismissal — includes dishonesty/plagiarism/falsifying records, violating drug/alcohol policy, abusive language, endangering patients or students, felony conviction, and failure to follow program/CUNY rules.

First-semester Generic students: failing one nursing course lets you repeat it (a second failure means dismissal). Failing more than one of NURS 204, NURS 210, or NURS 101 in your first semester means automatic dismissal.

Dismissed students must meet with the Chairperson within 10 days, then may appeal to the APR Committee within 5 days of that meeting, then to the School of Health Sciences and Professional Programs within 5 business days of a certified dismissal letter.

Testing Day Rules

Remote proctoring is mandatory for all nursing course exams, in-person or remote, per accreditation requirements.

  • Missed exam? One make-up per course, at faculty's discretion and format. Miss the make-up too and you get a zero. All make-ups happen at semester's end.
  • Late arrival: no extra time given. More than 15 minutes late and you're not allowed into the testing room at all.
  • Log in with your password 24 hours before arriving, to confirm exam access ahead of time.
  • Timing: 60 minutes for a 50-question exam, 120 minutes for a 100-question exam. Backtracking to change an answered question is not permitted.
  • No electronics in the testing area — phones fully off (not just vibrate) and stored up front. No smart watches. Only faculty-distributed calculators.
  • Seating is randomly assigned. Scratch paper must be returned immediately at the end.
  • All personal items (bags, books, food/drink, gum, lip balm, coats, etc.) go to the front of the room. A valid photo ID may be requested.

Restroom Policy

  • Go before the exam starts.
  • 50-item unit exams: no leaving the testing area to use the restroom.
  • Final exams: restroom only if accompanied by a faculty/staff member.

After the Exam

Log off immediately. Disclosing exam content before/during/after is an Academic Integrity violation. To review a graded exam, request it within 2 weeks of completion. Final exams aren't available for review until grades/attendance post; instructors hand-score finals for any student who failed the course.

Clinical Requirements

CPR, health clearance, CastleBranch, background checks, COVID policy, and Infection Control Certification. Full checklist and deadlines are on the Start Here page.

Health clearance is mandatory every semester. The Medical-Physical exam and immunization forms must be completed by a physician (signed and stamped) annually and uploaded to CastleBranch. All requirements must be met before the semester starts, or it results in an unexcused absence — no exceptions. Missing clinical requirement deadlines can mean delayed progression or dismissal.

Nursing students must also demonstrate Essential Competencies for Safe Clinical Practice — communication skills, critical thinking, and good moral character (no felony/misdemeanor convictions, no relevant disciplinary actions) — required for both applicants and current students.

Academic Integrity & AI Policy

Academic dishonesty (cheating, plagiarism, collusion, sabotage, falsifying records) is prohibited CUNY-wide and punishable up to suspension/expulsion. Instructors are required to report suspected dishonesty to the Department Chair and Dean of Students — this becomes part of your permanent file. A penalty grade from a dishonesty finding cannot be dropped. Full policy: York College Academic Integrity Policy.

AI Policy

Generative AI (including but not limited to ChatGPT) may not be used for any assignment or situation where the output could be interpreted as your own work, unless a faculty member or preceptor explicitly permits it. This policy is reviewed continuously against York/CUNY policy.

Withdrawal Policy (W vs. WU, deadlines)
  • First 3 weeks: drop a course via Schedule Builder — it won't appear on your transcript.
  • Weeks 4–10: withdraw via Schedule Builder — a W grade appears on the transcript.
  • After week 10: you must petition the Committee on Academic Policy and Standards (Room AC-4G04) to retroactively withdraw. Skip this and you'll get a WU — no GPA impact, but it marks an unofficial withdrawal.
Financial aid warning: a W or WU grade can affect your financial aid award. Talk to a Financial Aid Advisor (Room 1M08) before withdrawing, at any point in the semester.

See the Academic Calendar for the current-semester withdrawal deadline.

Appeals Policy & APR Committee Process

You can appeal a grade, denial of admission, the right to repeat a pre-screening/ prerequisite course or the pre-admission exam, or a dismissal. Submit your appeal in writing to the Nursing Admission, Progression, and Retention (APR) Committee, Science Building, Room 110C. It's logged, forwarded to the APR chair for a recommendation, then voted on by Department of Nursing faculty. You'll get a written decision during the academic semester.

The Committee on Academic Policy and Standards (Room 4G04, 718-262-2770) is a separate, college-wide committee that handles: late withdrawal, leave of absence, INC extension, appeal of dismissal, readmission, grade change (with instructor input), grade appeal (without instructor input), and appeal of denial. All petitions need written documentation of the reason; the committee sets the timeline.

See the Forms Library for the actual appeal/petition letters.

You don't have to go through this alone Beyond the formal process above, there's a real pattern of students banding together informally after failing a course (commonly Pharm or Med-Surg) to share appeal experiences and support each other. The Nursing Club is a good place to find that kind of peer support in addition to the official channels. Also worth knowing: appeals have been pursued right up through the first day of the next semester — if you're still working on one, it's not automatically too late.
