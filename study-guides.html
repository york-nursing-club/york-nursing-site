Study Guides

Organized by nursing semester, not by academic year — Semester 1 is your first term of NURS coursework, not necessarily your first year at York. This covers the Generic BS clinical phase only (the professional nursing courses in the last two years of the program), since that's the sequence with clinical rotations and shared exams.

Mapping derived from the handbook's 4-Year Plan We built the semester groupings below directly from the Generic BS four-year plan table in the Student Handbook. If your cohort numbers these semesters differently, let an officer know and we'll correct it — this is meant to match how students actually talk about "Semester 3," not just a guess.
These are mostly empty right now. There's no existing study guide content to pull from — this is a shell for the Club to fill in. If you have notes, practice questions, concept maps, or a study strategy that helped you pass a course, get it to a Club officer (see Directory) or drop a file in study-guides/ using the course code as the filename (e.g. NURS210.pdf) and it'll show up linked below.

2 credits

NURS 101 — Nursing Process and Pharmacology

Problem-solving approach to safe, accurate drug administration — dosage calculation, safety, client education, and the legal/ethical side of pharmacology. Weekly mandatory seminar.

RN-BS track

RN-BS students get transfer credit for most of the clinical-heavy courses above (NURS 310, 320, 340, 420, 430), so the semester breakdown looks different — mainly NURS 200, 203, 301, 314, 315, 405, 406, 407. Not broken out separately yet; ask an officer if you want this added.
